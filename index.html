A LITTLE ABOUT OUR INITIATIVE

Fit eCity is about facilitating random acts of play throughout Adelaide City Council including parklands, laneways &…anywhere, really. Exercise does not always spark excitement, so we incentivize play by allowing you to gain points towards discounts at various vendors around the Council. In collaboration with Adelaide City Council, University of South Australia – School of Health Sciences and Mitise Health & Fitness we are not only advising on ways to get and stay active, but we’re taking you by the hand and guiding your play time…randomly.

So, on your time or with a gentle push from Fit eCity play now or play later…play more!

The importance of physical activity in health and well-being is well known and for the most part well understood. But sometimes it’s hard to find time to get active. In my experience lack of time is the number one barrier that prevents people from participating in physical activity on a regular basis.

The good news is that even short burst of physical activity will help your health. While the World Health Organisation recommends that you accumulate at least 150 minutes of moderate- to vigorous-intensity aerobic physical activity per week, you can collect it in bouts of 10 minutes or more.

Jumping Jacks

Stand with your body weight balanced on one leg. Hold your arms out by your sides for balance. Repeatedly jump forward on one foot for a distance of 10 to 15 meters. Focus on horizontal distance with each hop, and make sure you hop continuously.
